The logo for Midwest Waste is a strong, distinctive mark that communicates effort and reliability. However, as your business grows and shifts more toward digital interactions, the current design presents some challenges in legibility and versatility.

Below is an analysis of your current logo, followed by a proposed **Brand Standard Guide** to help unify your visual identity across your trucks, website, and digital platforms.

### ---

**Part 1: Logo Analysis**

The "Bear and Boulder" concept is an excellent metaphor for the strength and diligence required in the waste industry.

#### **Strengths**

* **High Contrast:** The white-on-navy color scheme is professional and provides excellent visibility, especially on large-scale vehicle wraps.  
* **Dynamic Action:** The bear "pushing" the circle creates a sense of movement and progress, which separates it from more static, "industrial-only" waste logos.  
* **Brand Recognition:** Because it's already on your fleet, the silhouette of the bear has become your primary "brand equity."

#### **Areas for Improvement (Digital & Scalability)**

* **Small Text Legibility:** The words "EST 2015" and "CONSULTANTS" are very thin. In digital formats (like a mobile website header or a LinkedIn profile picture), these words disappear entirely.  
* **Aspect Ratio:** The logo is roughly square/tall. This makes it difficult to fit into horizontal website navigation bars without making the text look tiny.  
* **Color Balance:** The orange accent lines are very thin. They don't provide much visual weight and can often "vibrate" or look blurry on low-resolution screens.

### ---

**Part 2: Brand Standard Guide (Proposed)**

This guide ensures that anyone—from a web developer to a printer—uses your brand elements consistently.

#### **1\. Color Palette**

To maintain a professional yet energetic feel, we define the "Midwest Navy" and "Consultant Orange."

| Element | Hex Code | Usage |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Primary Navy** | \#1B365D | Backgrounds, Headers, Primary Text |
| **Action Orange** | \#FF8200 | Call-to-Action buttons, Accents, Highlight lines |
| **Pure White** | \#FFFFFF | Logo mark, Body background |
| **Steel Gray** | \#F4F4F4 | Secondary backgrounds, subtle borders |

#### **2\. Typography**

A brand’s voice is often heard through its fonts.

* **Primary Font (Headers/Logo):** **Montserrat (Bold)**. This is a geometric sans-serif that mirrors the "circular" nature of your logo. It is highly readable on screens.  
* **Secondary Font (Body Text):** **Open Sans**. A clean, friendly font that works well for long paragraphs of service descriptions on your website.

#### **3\. Logo Variations & Usage**

Since you cannot change the trucks, we should create **Digital-First Variations** that keep the bear but rearrange the text for better performance.

* **The "Primary" (Vehicle Style):** The bear pushing the circle. Best for large print and square profile photos.  
* **The "Horizontal" (Website Header):** The bear icon on the left, with "MIDWEST WASTE" in large type to the right. This allows the text to be much larger and more legible.  
* **The "Icon" (Favicon/App):** Just the white bear silhouette or the circle with "MW" inside. This is for the tiny 16x16 pixel icon in browser tabs.

#### **4\. Clear Space & Integrity**

To ensure the logo remains professional, it must "breathe."

* **Safe Zone:** Always maintain a clear space around the logo equal to the width of the bear’s head.  
* **Don'ts:** Do not stretch the logo, do not change the bear’s color to orange, and do not place the logo on a busy photographic background without a solid color "container."

### ---

**Part 3: Digital Implementation Strategies**

To improve your presentation without rebranding your physical trucks, implement these "Digital-Only" refinements:

1. **Bold the "Consultants" Text:** On your website, use a version of the logo where "CONSULTANTS" is slightly thicker (Medium weight instead of Light). This won't be a "change" to the logo's soul, but it will make it readable.  
2. **Simplify for Social Media:** Most social platforms use a circular crop. The current logo is a "square inside a circle." I recommend using a version for Facebook/Instagram that removes "EST 2015" and "CONSULTANTS" entirely, leaving just the Bear and "MIDWEST WASTE" for maximum impact.  
3. **The "Orange Line" Fix:** In the digital version, increase the thickness of those two orange bars by 25%. It makes the logo feel more "grounded" and ensures the orange pops against the navy blue on mobile screens.  
4. **Use SVG Files:** Ensure your web developer is using .svg (Vector) files rather than .png or .jpg. This ensures the bear's silhouette stays perfectly sharp no matter how much a user zooms in on their phone.
